Our Approach

Our focus is to help individuals minimise joint & muscle related pain & stiffness. This allows to regain maximum movement, efficiency and flexibility.

Our approach is aimed at identifying and treating the root cause of the pain and in turn relieving symptoms and preventing further injury. Muscles and Joints form a complex interconnected web. Tightness or weakness in any area of the body can affect the way we move and perform. If left untreated these factors can lead to further problems elsewhere in the body.

Rehabilitation

During your initial appointment we will take a medical history and examine you to assess and evaluate your injury. Once we have a diagnosis, we will create a tailor-made treatment program to addresses the root cause of the pain. We will start the treatment straight away.

Each client we see will have a unique injury or issue, treatment will be tailored to their needs. This will usually involve a combination of the therapies. We specialize in soft tissue massage, spinal and peripheral mobilisations, dry needling, taping, exercise programs and postural re-education.

Pilates

Modified Pilates is an excellent form of exercises for clients with musculoskeletal problems, poor posture and sports injuries. Used in combination with other therapies, modified Pilates can help to strengthen any weak areas in the body, reducing the chance of reoccurring a recurrence of symptoms.

A 1-to-1 60 minute session is £60

Remedial Massage

Sports and Remedial Massage is the great way of alleviating muscle tension, joint stiffness and relieve pain. The benefits are often immediate.Regular massage can help to avoid injuries caused by overuse and maintain good muscle flexibility and health.

The Rehabilitation Specialists

The Sport Rehab is run by Aga Waszkielis in collaboration with a group of expert rehabilitators.

Aga studied Sport Rehabilitation at St. Marys University College, London. Since beginning her career in 2007, Aga has helped many with joint and muscle pain or a sports related injury to regain full mobility and function. Most recently she has worked with the Ladies European Golf Tour.

Aga has always enjoyed sport. She started playing handball at the age of 12 and while studying at the Academy of Physical Education in Warsaw, Poland played at national club level. She became passionate about exercise and its role in maintaining healthy body. She went on to specialise the use of exercise as a tool in rehabilitation and in maintaining muscular balance, function and flexibility.

She now specializes in treating injuries with a holistic approach, pinpointing the root cause of the pain and enabling clients to reach their maximum potential. Aga treats clients, children as well as adults with sport related and non sports-related issues. She works comprehensively with her clients to overcome pain or disability caused by injury. She also teaches them how to prevent further injury.
